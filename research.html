Research

I study satellite galaxies of hosts with low stellar mass, between the Large Magellanic Cloud and the Milky Way. These galaxies allow us to further understand hierarchical structure formation, where small structures merged together to form the large objects we see today, and also understand what the universe is made of.

Satellite Luminosity Function of Low-Mass Host Galaxies

Recently, I used the COSMOS survey to study satellite galaxy populations of hosts with stellar mass 9.5 < log(M_*/M_⊙) < 10.5 at redshifts 0.1 < z < 0.8. Using a bayesian statistical analysis, we were able to measure the hosts satellite luminosity function (number of satellites per host as a function of magnitude) by detecting a satellite overdensity signal above that of the background/foreground objects.

This is the first study of low-mass host satellite systems across cosmic time! And is important because it allows us to test hierarchical galaxy formation in ΛCDM, the current cosmology that supports our models of galaxy formation.
